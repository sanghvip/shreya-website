The philosophy

The feeling always
comes first

Most approaches to personal change start in the wrong place: the rational mind. 'Change your thinking and your behaviour will follow.' This is almost never true — because behaviour does not live in the rational brain. It lives in the nervous system, in the emotional patterns formed long before you had words for them.

The Neurom approach starts where you actually are — in a feeling state. The F-B-T Link (Feeling → Behaviour → Thought), developed through years of practice, reflects a simple insight: when we meet the feeling first, the behaviour becomes navigable. When the behaviour shifts, the thought transforms naturally. Not through effort. Through understanding.

Neurom is also built on the belief that culturally fluent mental health is not a luxury — it is a clinical necessity. For South Asian Canadians navigating the intersection of family expectations, immigration stress, identity pressure, and professional ambition, a practitioner who understands both the clinical framework and the cultural context changes everything.

NLP Presupposition 01
The map is not the territory

Your experience of reality is your map of it — drawn by your history, your nervous system, your language. The map is not reality. Maps can be redrawn. When you feel stuck, you are not stuck in reality — you are stuck in your current map of it. NLP is the practice of updating the map.

NLP Presupposition 02
Every behaviour has a positive intention

The anger, the avoidance, the people-pleasing, the inner critic — every pattern, however painful or self-destructive it appears, is protecting something or meeting a need. This is not an excuse. It is information. When you understand what a behaviour is protecting you from, you can find a better way to meet that need.

NLP Presupposition 03
There is no failure — only feedback

You did not fail. You got a result that was not the one you wanted. That result tells you something about what needs to change. This reframe — from failure to feedback — is one of the most practically liberating ideas in all of NLP. It is also rigorously supported by the learning science.

NLP Presupposition 04
You have all the resources you need

You are not broken and you are not missing something. The work is about accessing what is already within your lived experience — not installing something new, but finding what was always there. This is why NLP begins with your best memories, your peak states, your own existing emotional intelligence.

NLP Presupposition 05
The meaning of communication is the response you get

If someone consistently misunderstands you, the NLP approach is not 'they're not listening.' It is 'what can I do differently?' Not because you are always wrong, but because communication is a feedback loop, and you are responsible for your half of it. Quietly revolutionary in every relationship.

The process

What happens
when you work with us

Every session follows a deliberate structure — not a script, but a framework that ensures the work goes to the level where change actually happens.

01
The free intro call

15 minutes. No forms, no pressure. You tell us briefly what's going on. We explain what Neurom does and how. Both of us decide if it's the right fit. If it is — we book session one. If it isn't — we'll tell you honestly who we think would serve you better.

02
Session one: the feeling

The first session begins with the F-B-T Link: we start at the feeling. Not a questionnaire — a real conversation. What is the emotional state that is most getting in your way? Where do you feel it in your body? What does it protect you from? The session stays here until we understand it.

03
The NLP work

Once the feeling is understood, we introduce the specific NLP technique most relevant to what the session has revealed — anchoring, submodality shift, parts integration, reframing, the swish pattern, or timeline work. You experience it, not just understand it. You leave with a specific tool to practise before the next session.

04
The change between sessions

The 23 hours between sessions are where real change happens — in the moment of awareness, the micro-choice, the noticing. Every session ends with a specific homework practice. You bring what you noticed back to the next session. The work compounds. This is how lasting change is built — not in a room, but in real life.

A note on how long it takes

Some patterns shift noticeably in 3–4 sessions. Others — particularly deep attachment patterns, identity-level beliefs, or long-standing emotional responses — require 8–12 sessions to work properly. We will always be honest about what we think the work requires. We do not prolong sessions unnecessarily. We do not cut them short when they need more time.

The framework

The F-B-T Link —
Feeling, Behaviour, Thought

Most therapeutic and coaching models follow the Cognitive Triangle in the traditional sequence: Thought → Feeling → Behaviour. Change your thinking, and your emotions and behaviour will follow. This is theoretically sound — and practically unhelpful for the majority of people who seek it.

Because when you are triggered, flooded, anxious, or shut down — your rational mind is the last thing available to you. The prefrontal cortex, which mediates reasoning and perspective-taking, is literally less active when the limbic system is running. No amount of CBT worksheets can reach a nervous system in threat mode.

The F-B-T Link reverses the sequence deliberately. Start at the Feeling — acknowledge it, understand what it is communicating, meet it with curiosity rather than suppression. From that understanding, the Behaviour becomes navigable. From the behavioural shift, the Thought transforms naturally. Not through effort. Through alignment.

This is the insight that NLP has always operated on — even when it didn't name it this way. Every NLP technique (anchoring, submodality work, parts integration) works at the level of state before it works at the level of cognition. The F-B-T Link makes this explicit and teachable.

F
Feeling — the entry point

The feeling is not a problem to solve. It is a signal to understand. What is this emotion trying to tell you? What is it protecting? What need does it represent? NLP begins here — in the body, in the state, in the nervous system response — before offering any technique or reframe.

B
Behaviour — the visible pattern

Once the feeling is understood, the behaviour that flows from it becomes visible as a pattern rather than a character flaw. The avoidance, the outburst, the withdrawal, the people-pleasing — each has a structure. NLP tools work here: the swish pattern interrupts the automatic behaviour; parts work resolves the internal conflict; anchoring installs an alternative response.

T
Thought — the natural transformation

When the feeling has been met and the behaviour has shifted, thought transforms without effort — not through willpower but through a nervous system that is no longer in protection mode. The reframe that was intellectually available but emotionally impossible before now becomes obvious and felt. This is sustainable change.

NLP Anchoring — access peak states on demand

Used by athletes, executives, and performers worldwide. Set intentionally at the peak of a genuinely experienced positive state, fired reliably at the moments you most need access to your best self. One of the most practically transformative techniques in all of NLP.

Submodality work — change how memory feels

Your brain encodes every experience with qualities — size, brightness, distance, sound, movement. Changing those qualities changes how the experience affects you. Make the image smaller, darker, further away — the emotional charge reduces measurably. This is neuroplasticity, not magic.

Parts integration — resolving internal conflict

When part of you wants to change and part of you doesn't — this is where real change gets stuck. Parts work honours what both parts are trying to achieve and finds a new strategy that meets both sets of needs. Not compromise — synthesis. When it works, change becomes effortless.

The NLP meta-model — precision language

The meta-model is a set of questions that recover the specific truth beneath generalisations, deletions, and distortions in language. 'Nobody understands me' → 'Who specifically? In what situations? What have you tried?' Precision changes the cognitive landscape immediately.

The STOP process — interrupting trigger responses

Stop. Take a slow breath (longer exhale than inhale). Observe — where in your body is the tension? Proceed with intention. A four-step process for catching yourself between stimulus and response. Simple to learn. Transformative with practice.

Common questions

Everything you want

Genuine questions from real people — answered honestly, without marketing language or clinical evasion.

What actually happens in a Neurom NLP session?+
A Neurom session begins at the feeling — not with a questionnaire, an intake form, or a CBT worksheet. Using the F-B-T Link, we first understand the emotional state: what is it communicating? What is it protecting? Where is it felt in the body? Only once the feeling is understood do we introduce a specific NLP technique. The technique is chosen based on what the session reveals — not a predetermined script. You will leave with a written session summary, a specific practice to try before the next session, and a genuine shift that you can feel, not just understand.
Is this therapy, coaching, or something else?+
It is a careful integration of all three. Counselling psychology provides the depth, relational safety, and clinical framework. NLP provides specific, demonstrable state-changing techniques. Adult learning theory (from Shreya's George Brown College programme) provides the understanding of how adults change — through experiential learning, personal relevance, and reflective practice. If you are in active mental health crisis, experiencing psychosis, or need psychiatric assessment, Neurom will refer you to the appropriate clinical support. For the vast majority of people navigating stuck patterns, relationship challenges, confidence, burnout, or career transition, Neurom provides exactly the depth they need.
Do you offer sessions in Hindi?+
Yes. Sessions are available in English, Hindi, and a natural blend of both. For South Asian clients — particularly those discussing family dynamics, relationship patterns, cultural identity, or the specific pressure of izzat and family expectation — the ability to move between both languages in a single session is clinically significant. Some emotional content is simply more accurately expressed in the language it was originally experienced in. Neurom actively encourages this. You never have to translate yourself.
How is NLP different from CBT or regular talk therapy?+
CBT works primarily at the level of thought — changing cognitive patterns through identification, challenge, and replacement. It is evidence-based and genuinely useful for many people. NLP works primarily at the level of state and subconscious processing — it changes how experiences are encoded in the nervous system before addressing the cognitive layer. The F-B-T Link reflects this: Feeling before Thought. For people who have found CBT helpful but surface-level, or who understand their patterns cognitively but find them unchanged behaviourally, NLP often addresses the layer that CBT didn't reach. Talk therapy, while valuable, typically does not involve the specific technical interventions NLP offers — the live demonstrations, the anchoring, the submodality work. Neurom integrates all three where appropriate.
What is the Neurom Circle and who is it for?+
The Neurom Circle is a free monthly community gathering held on the last Sunday of every month (4–6pm) at rotating venues across downtown Toronto. It is not therapy, it is not a workshop, and it is not a sales pitch for individual sessions. It is a community of practice — a guided gathering for people who want to think more clearly, connect more honestly, and go home with something they can actually use. The format: 20-minute presentation on the month's psychological theme (follows Neurom's content calendar), 40 minutes of facilitated small-group conversation, 30 minutes of informal connection with tea and coffee. Free entry or $10 suggested donation. Open to everyone. No prior experience with NLP or counselling required.
Is NLP backed by scientific evidence?+
The honest answer: the NLP evidence base is mixed. The foundational research was conducted in the 1970s with methodology that wouldn't pass peer review today, and some systematic reviews have found limited evidence for specific NLP claims. What is also true is that many individual NLP techniques draw directly on principles that are robustly supported in adjacent research areas — somatic anchoring draws on Pavlovian conditioning, submodality work draws on the well-established neuroscience of memory reconsolidation, parts integration draws on Internal Family Systems theory, and state management draws on polyvagal regulation research. Neurom uses NLP within a broader therapeutic framework that takes the clinical evidence seriously. We will always be honest about what the research does and does not show — and our commitment is to what demonstrably works in the room, not what sounds good on paper.
Do you offer a sliding scale for lower-income clients?+
Yes. Neurom offers a limited number of sliding-scale spots for clients referred through COSTI, Punjabi Community Health Services, India Rainbow Community Services, and other settlement agencies in the GTA. If you have been referred by a settlement organisation, please mention this in your contact form. For private individuals who are experiencing genuine financial hardship, please reach out directly — we will always try to find a way to make sessions accessible. The community workshops and Neurom Circle are free or by donation for this reason: we believe access to psychological tools should not be exclusively available to those who can afford premium pricing.
How do corporate workshops differ from standard EAP?+
Standard EAP (Employee Assistance Programmes) typically offers 3–6 confidential counselling sessions to employees who self-refer. The utilisation rate across Canadian organisations averages 5–10%. Among South Asian employees, this drops significantly due to cultural stigma and the perception that Western therapeutic models don't understand their context. Neurom's corporate offering is fundamentally different: culturally adapted (available in English and Hindi, understanding of South Asian workplace dynamics), clinically grounded (NLP + counselling + adult learning, not wellness platitudes), actively facilitated (sessions go to people, not just available to them), and measurably designed (Bloom's Taxonomy, interactive formats, specific tools to take away). We work with HR teams to make mental health genuinely accessible to the employees who most need it — not just the ones already comfortable seeking it.
