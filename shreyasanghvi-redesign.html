§ 02 · The F-B-T Approach

The feeling always
comes first.

Most therapy starts with the thought. We start with the feeling. The deepest behaviours do not live in the rational brain — they live in the nervous system, in patterns formed long before you had language to describe them.

The F-B-T Approach reverses the sequence: Feeling → Behaviour → Thought. When the body is met first, the rest follows. Anchoring, parts work, submodality shifts, the meta-model — the techniques are NLP-grounded, but the philosophy is older. It is what every grandmother who calmed you down already knew.

§ 04 · The practitioner

Hi, I'm Shreya.

I am a certified NLP practitioner, ICF coach, and counsellor based in Toronto. I trained first in India, where I founded and led the Learning & Talent Development practice at Psychologia Connect — India's first web-based psychology services platform. I now work with individuals, couples, and organisations across the GTA and online across Ontario.

When I moved from India to Canada, I lived first-hand what many of my clients are navigating — the quiet disorientation of rebuilding professional identity in a new country, the gap between who you were and who you are permitted to be here, the weight of family expectations carried across borders. That transition is not just what I counsel. It is what I have lived.
